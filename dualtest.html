The crisis

Maternal syphilis is deeply neglected — and getting worse

Each year, over 1 million pregnant women have active syphilis. Most adverse outcomes occur among women who attend prenatal care but simply aren't tested. Maternal syphilis causes more stillbirths and newborn deaths than child deaths from HIV — yet receives a fraction of the funding.

The solution

One test, one shot — deployed at scale

01 — Screen

Dual HIV/syphilis test

A single fingerstick yields results within 15 minutes. Can be administered by midwives, nurses, and community volunteers. Deploys within existing HIV infrastructure without compromising HIV testing accuracy.

Just 15¢ more than HIV-only tests
02 — Treat

Single penicillin injection

A single shot administered before a woman's third trimester dramatically reduces the risk of adverse birth outcomes. Same-day treatment is possible — no follow-up visits required.

Less than 50¢ per treatment
03 — Scale

Government partnership model

Evidence Action provides technical assistance across five core areas: piloting, policy, training, procurement, supply chain management, and data monitoring — setting governments on track for sustained ownership.
