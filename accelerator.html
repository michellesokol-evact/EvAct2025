The Accelerator Model: Why Systematic Vetting Matters

Our six-stage framework evaluates interventions across 12 criteria organized into three tiers. Primary criteria examine evidence quality, cost-effectiveness, and scale of potential impact. Secondary criteria assess organizational fit, fundability, government landscape, and implementation feasibility. Tertiary criteria consider transformational potential, replicability, and attribution.

This comprehensive approach requires investment. Our annual Accelerator budget of approximately $4 million supports the staffing, in-country scoping, and Stage 4 field testing that moves interventions from promising concepts to implementation-ready programs. This includes:

  • Rigorous evidence review to ensure interventions demonstrate effectiveness in controlled research settings
  • Cost-effectiveness modeling that accounts for real-world implementation costs, not just commodity prices
  • In-country scoping to evaluate government engagement, partner landscape, implementation feasibility, and data availability
  • Stage 4 field testing through formative research, prototyping, and piloting that pressure-tests key program assumptions before full-scale commitment

The result: programs like Syphilis-Free Start and Equal Vitamin Access that now reach tens of millions of people with proven, cost-effective interventions. These successes validate an approach that prioritizes careful vetting over rapid deployment.
