Shampande Clinic: A Game-Changer in Maternal Health

Shampande Clinic in Choma District, Southern Province, has emerged as a model site following the introduction of the HIV/Syphilis Dual Rapid Diagnostic Test, as part of Zambia's strategic efforts to strengthen integrated disease surveillance and maternal health services.

Before Implementation

Syphilis testing required sending samples to labs or conducting manual tests in the MCH Department using reagents and a manual shaker. This led to:

  • 24-hour wait times for results
  • Missed critical treatment windows
  • Limited support from Community-Based Volunteers
  • Time-consuming processes prone to errors

The Transformation

"The new way of testing is much easier, because you don't have to prick the patient twice. Both tests can be performed using just one test kit," said Clement, one of the CBVs.
